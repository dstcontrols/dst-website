A CIO's critical challenge: Real-Time Data for Real-Time Decision Making
Real-Time, Enterprise-Wide
While corporate IT happily enables exponential growth in transactional data available to its customers in the corporate suite, internal sources of high value data are often overlooked.

Volumes of plant-floor data—even when processed by data-warehouse, supply chain, and ERP systems—often only appear as "rolled-up" summary information like averages and daily totals. The downside? Executives can’t easily access that data at anywhere near original resolutions.

For example, Executive Management wants to analyze staffing patterns and shift-change options for a new product line; but IT says, Sorry, the information you need is buried (siloed!) as raw data in a historian, over in Operations.

Management needs easier access to plant data for business intelligence—and real-time data is critical for meaningful KPI’s and compliance alerts.

As a result, savvy IT departments and Operations Technology groups are dropping their shields and upping their data-collection capabilities with real-time data infrastructures supplied by companies like OSIsoft, Rockwell, Invensys, and GE—all integrated by DST Controls.

The Convergence of IT and OT Data
A major barrier to converging data typically managed by IT and data from process and manufacturing sources, is that IT departments are sometimes not as experienced with Operational Technology (and real-time data) as they are with traditional office-level IT. What happens on the production side is traditionally not part of their domain.

IT staffers may administer IP addresses, upgrade server software and maintain firewalls, but key production systems such as SCADA, HMI, plant historians, environmental monitors, equipment interfaces and other critical plant infrastructure remain under operations management.
